My definition of "craft."

According to the Merriam Webster dictionary, the word “craft” means skill in making things, especially with the hands. Craft relates to the practice of graphic design because it relates to the process of creating and making. Craft can mean painting, drawing, sculpting, building, folding, and pasting. I love doing crafts, especially since I was a child - making things was my favorite hobby. If I could do crafts my entire life, I could. That is why I chose graphic design as my career and I am so grateful for that. Graphic design allows me to practice craft when I combine digital with analog elements. Whenever I take a picture of something I made analogically and then put it in a digital format, it adds an unexpected element, such as texture and depth, that brings the design to life. Another example of practicing craft is when I made a candy box out of paper and candy wrappers for the last project for Sophomore Studio. Additionally, I practice craft when we as designers design, print, and bind using different techniques such as saddle-stitch, sewn, spiral, and Japanese Stab Binding. A craft that I hope to try more with combining analog and digital, is printmaking. I love carving linoleum blocks and printing - the texture and color looks so good! Crafting analog adds a special quality to a design piece that you can never really recreate digitally.
