Purpose is the reason why something has been done. Everything has purpose in life and especially in art and design. Every line you put on, every color that you use, or even every typeface that you use to create a design is so significant that you need to think about each action you make and have a purpose behind every action. I learned that designing with purpose is so important in order for me to actually fall in love with the project. I want my designs to have a purpose of my own to make it unique to myself. I have an easier time to grow an emotional connection with my design when I design with purpose. Also, once you have a meaningful purpose, the motivation automatically comes along with it because it means something to you and you get excited to communicate that to the world using design. Yes, designs can seem to be purely decorative sometimes, but behind every design, everyone has a purpose. Thinking about the purpose behind every action is hard and thatâ€™s why brainstorming ideas for a project is the hardest than actually executing it. Showing the meaning and the purpose through simplicity of design is the biggest challenge that I face as a designer. In the end, craftsmanship, creativity, and the quality of work is what is viewed initially, but the purpose of it is what makes a work worth more and how it connects the artist to the audience better.

My purpose in life is create. Not only because I am a graphic design major, I love to create things in life whether it is related to art or in general. I love to create art, relationships with people, and better life everyday.
